The "Why"

By integrating the knowledge acquired in this page with one's thought process, the reader may be able to better understand themselves, the people around them, their own habits and beliefs.

That understanding may then be used to make changes within themselves and their surroundings to allow themselves to grow in a healthier manner.

For better comprehension of things, the reader should find more information related to the addressed topics from scholarly sources beyond this page, and reflect over it.

Metacognition

  • Knowledge and understanding of one's own thinking.
  • Awareness or analysis of one's own learning or thinking processes.
  • Awareness of one's own cognitive processes, often involving a conscious attempt to control them.

Components

  • Metacognitive knowledge.
  • Metacognitive experience.
  • Metacognitive monitoring and regulation.


    • Metacognitive Knowledge

  • Personal knowledge.

    • (hobbies, memory characteristics, ways of thinking, ability limitations)

  • Task knowledge.

    • (task structures, task goals)

  • Strategic knowledge.

    • (advantages, disadvantages, applicability of strategies)


    Metacognitive Experience

    An individual's subjective perception of the level of difficulty of particular cognitive operations such as that of remembering, learning, language use, problem solving, decision making, reasoning.


    Monitoring & Regulation

    The ability to plan, monitor, and evaluate one's own cognitive activities and regulate cognitive processes such as adjusting task goals, distributing learning time, selecting cognitive strategies helps improve one's own performance in many ways such as better use of attentional resources and existing strategies, a greater awareness of comprehension breakdowns.
